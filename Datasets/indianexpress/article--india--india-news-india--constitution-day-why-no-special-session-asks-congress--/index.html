Constitution day: Why no special session, asks Congress

According to sources, Congress leader Mallikarjun Kharge expressed his party’s reservation about the government’s “unilateral” decision.

By: Express News Service | New Delhi | Published:November 26, 2015 1:58 am

At the Lok Sabha Speaker’s all-party meeting on Wednesday evening, Congress objected to the government’s decision of holding a discussion on ‘Commitment to the Constitution’ as a part of the session, instead of calling a special session.

The Congress argued that the discussion, which has been listed for Thursday and Friday in both Houses as a part of celebrations to mark the 125th birth anniversary of Dr B R Ambedkar, should have been held as part of a special session with the Presidential address as its part.

According to sources, Congress leader Mallikarjun Kharge expressed his party’s reservation about the government’s “unilateral” decision.
