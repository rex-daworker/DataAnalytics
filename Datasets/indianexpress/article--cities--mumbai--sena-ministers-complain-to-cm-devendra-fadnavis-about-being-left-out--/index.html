Sena ministers complain to CM Devendra Fadnavis about being ‘left out’

According to the minister, at the Indu Mills function and Bandra Kurla Complex rally, the government had earmarked seats for Sena ministers.

Ministers from the Sena expressed their displeasure at not being consulted for the event to Chief Minister Devendra Fadnavis on Wednesday.

It was the sidelining of  Shiv Sena ministers by the BJP in the decision-making and planning of the foundation stone laying ceremony for Ambedkar memorial that appears to have been a sore point with the Sena. Prime Minister Narendra Modi had himself performed the bhoomi poojan for the memorial.

Ministers from the Sena expressed their displeasure at not being consulted for the event to Chief Minister Devendra Fadnavis on Wednesday. After the weekly cabinet meeting on the day, Industries Minister and senior Sena leader Subash Desai led a delegation from the party to meet Fadnavis.

In the 30-member Maharashtra cabinet, the Sena has a representation of 10, while the rest are from BJP. “We are very hurt as the BJP did not involve us in the decision-making and planning of the Ambedkar memorial event at Indu Mills. It was a very significant decision as Prime Minister Narendra Modi performed the bhoomi pujan. But none of our ministers in the Fadnavis government were consulted on the matter,” Desai said, adding that the chief minister has assured them to look into the matter.

The Sena had boycotted the function with party president Uddhav Thackeray touring drought-hit Beed to meet farmers. None of the Sena ministers whose names had been shortlisted in the invitation attended the function either.
However, a senior BJP minister rubbished the Sena’s claims and said that their decision to boycott the Ambedkar memorial function has exposed their anti-Dalit agenda and has not gone well down with Dalit organisations. “So, they are now raking up issues to blame the BJP.” he said. Even Dalit leaders in the Sena said that Thackeray should have attended the Ambedkar memorial bhoomi pujan.

“The Shiv Sena’s opposition to the reservation policy is well known. In 1994, the Sena had opposed renaming Marathwada University after BR Ambedkar. Now, the boycott of Ambedkar memorial has dented its image among Dalits,” the minister said.

“Our chief minister has  always believed in coalition dharma. Fadnavis had specially asked Desai to accompany
him on all foreign tours to attract investments in Maharashtra. The Ambedkar memorial was a collective cabinet decision where the Sena was present and gave its consent. How can they complain now? It was their decision to skip the bhoomi pujan despite the CM’s invitation. Our senior minister for industries Prakash Mehta personally visited Matoshree to invite Uddhav Thackeray,” the minister added.

According to the minister, at the Indu Mills function and Bandra Kurla Complex rally, the government had earmarked seats for Sena ministers. “Their names and pictures were put on hoardings, but had to be removed after they conveyed their decision to boycott the events,” he said.
