Rain brings mercury down, power situation improves

On Thursday, the maximum temperature was 41.1 degree Celsius.

By: Express News Service | New Delhi | Updated: June 14, 2014 3:07 pm

Power crisis might be over for now, but water woes persist. A protest in the capital on Friday. (Express Photo: Prem Nath Pandey)

Overnight rain, measuring 3.6 mm until 8.30 am on Friday, brought a sharp dip in temperature with the mercury reaching a maximum of 36.4 degree Celsius, three degrees below normal, by afternoon. The minimum temperature was recorded at a pleasant 22.7 degree Celsius.

This is the first time in over a week that the maximum temperature settled below the 40-degree mark. On Thursday, the maximum temperature was 41.1 degree Celsius. The rain broke the intense heatwave spell and put an end to the long power outages the capital has been suffering over the past several days. Weatherman said the temperature is likely to remain around 40 degree Celsius on Saturday.

“There would be mainly clear sky on Saturday with maximum and minimum likely to remain around 40 and 28 degrees, respectively,” a MeT official said.  With the dip in temperature, the power supply demand too went down. “The peak demand is on a downward trend since June 12. On June 13, the peak demand was 4,689 MW, which is 781 MW less than the June 11 peak demand of 5,470 MW. On the supply side, there are no constraints and sufficient power is available,” a statement issued by the Delhi government said.

The total demand during the 24 hours from midnight of June 11 till midnight of June 12 was 108.308 Million Units, of which 107.047 Million Units was successfully met, the Power department said.

“In the early hours of June 13,  between 1.30 am and 2.30 am, loadshedding to the tune of about 370MW was carried out due to intermittent thunderstorms with high velocity winds. However, the system was normalised by 3.15 am.  During the peak consumption hours on Friday, due to decrease in temperature, the discoms did not have rotational loadshedding.

Source: Indian Meteorological Department
