Recap: All our iPhone 6s, iPad Pro, and Apple TV stories in one place

Catch up on anything you missed this week as you wait to preorder your new iPhone 6s.

Comments
Susie Ochs | @sfsooz
Executive Editor, Macworld
  • Sep 11, 2015 7:00 PM

It was a huge week in the Apple-verse, with Wednesday’s special event packing in the new iPhones, the powerful iPad Pro, and the brand-new Apple TV. There was even a pretty big update to the iPad mini that barely got mentioned since the rest of the keynote was so packed. Plus, next week brings the launch of iOS 9, so there’s a lot to be excited about!

And we are definitely excited.

Tim Cook does a little recap at the end of every Apple special event, so we’re going to borrow a page from his playbook here. This list contains every story we wrote about this week’s news—perfect for catching up on anything you may have missed while you’re waiting to preorder your iPhone 6s.

iPhone 6s and 6s Plus

  • Hands-on with the iPhone 6s and 6s Plus

    “The only thing that’s changed is everything,” Apple said about the new iPhone 6s and iPhone 6s Plus at Wednesday’s Apple event. That’s both true and untrue. The case looks pretty much the same, the screen sizes didn’t change, and the entry-level model still has a paltry 16GB of storage. But inside it’s an all-new iPhone with some impressive new capabilities.

  • iPhone 6s and 6s Plus FAQ: The complete guide to Apple’s new phones

    While they might look the same, the new iPhones are made of Apple’s 7000 series aluminum alloy, the strongest of any iPhone ever made, which is also what Apple used to craft the Apple Watch Sport. The aluminum is aerospace industry-grade.

  • How, when, and where to preorder the iPhone 6s and 6s Plus

    Apple’s website isn’t the only place you can preorder a new phone. The Apple Store app for iOS is often a faster and smoother way to place an order, in our experience, and Apple recommends that route, too.

  • Why ‘S year’ iPhones are a bigger deal than they may seem

    Like the other “S” models that came before it, the iPhone 6s is much more than the sum of its improvements.

  • New iPhones are here: iPhone 6s and 6s Plus debut with new 3D Touch displays

  • How does the iPhone 6s camera stack up against other smartphones?

  • 3D Touch FAQ: Here’s the scoop on the iPhone 6s’ new pressure-sensitive display

  • The iPhone Upgrade Program is all about keeping you in Apple’s ecosystem

  • iPhone 6s pricing plan offers frequent upgrades and AppleCare+ for a premium

  • Sprint and T-Mobile remind you about their iPhone 6s deals

  • T-Mobile wants you to buy an iPhone 6s for $20 a month

  • Can your iPhone 6 case fit the new iPhone 6s?

  • The week in iPhone cases: The first iPhone 6s and 6s Plus cases are here

Apple TV

  • Hands-on with the new Apple TV and Siri Remote

    Now we’re talking—the Siri Remote makes voice search a piece of cake.

  • Apple TV FAQ: Apple’s new streaming video and gaming machine explained

    Apps should be as diverse as what’s available in iOS. Obviously, video-streaming services are front and center, and Apple has commitments from big names in television program creation and movie licensing, like ABC, Disney, HBO, Hulu, and Showtime.

  • New Apple TV: Siri and the App Store are the stars of Apple’s new set-top box

  • Apple TV drops optical audio-out: How to pump up the volume again

  • Why the Apple TV won’t revolutionize gaming

  • Apple’s tvOS: Can HomeKit apps be far behind?

iPad Pro and iPad mini 4

  • Hands-on with iPad Pro and Apple Pencil, built for getting stuff done

    But as large as it is, the iPad Pro doesn’t feel unwieldy, even to me, an avowed fan of the iPad mini. We’ve learned from the iMac and the iPhone that people love giant screens with tons of pixels crammed on to them, and that’s what the iPad Pro delivers, along with performance that should let more people than ever leave their laptops at home.

  • Hands-on with the iPad Pro’s Smart Keyboard

    Would I want to write a novel on the Smart Keyboard or the iPad Pro’s touchscreen keyboard? No, I suppose I wouldn’t. But I’m pretty sure that I could.

  • iPad Pro FAQ: Everything you need to know about Apple’s 12.9-inch mega tablet

    The iPad Pro fills up its large screen with 5.6 million pixels in a 2732x2048 resolution (versus the 2048x1536 resolution of the iPad Air 2), making it the highest resolution Retina display Apple has ever produced, even besting the resolution of the 15-inch MacBook Pro.

  • Everything you need to know about iPad mini 4, a thinner, lighter, multitasking machine

    The biggest change by far is the 4th-gen tablet’s ability to support all of iOS 9’s multitasking features: Slide Over, Picture in Picture, and Split View, a tool that was exclusive to the iPad Air 2 prior to Wednesday’s launch event.

  • iPad Pro revealed with Smart Keyboard and Apple Pencil

  • Oops! Adobe’s iPad Pro press release points to 4GB of RAM

  • Video: Apple iPad Pro sports 12.9-inch screen

iOS 9 and El Capitan

  • Mark your calendar: Apple announces OS X El Capitan and iOS 9 release dates

  • El Capitan FAQ: Everything you need to know about OS X 10.11

  • Take secure steps in upgrading to iOS 9

  • iOS 9 beta bring a taco emoji to iPhones at long last

Elsewhere

  • Apple’s Sept 9 event recap: All the highlights, zingers, and product details you may have missed

    Apple CEO Tim Cook kicked off the event on Wednesday morning and wasted no time in getting down to business. He immediately began talking about the success of the Apple Watch and where the platform was going with watchOS 2, which drops on September 16.

  • Apple announces new Apple Watch bands and finishes, and watchOS 2 ships Sept 16

    Apple also announced new Apple Watch bands and finishes, available today in 24 countries, and showed off a partnership with luxury-goods maker Hermes to bring its Double Tour bands—along with classic Hermes faces unique to those bands—to the Watch platform.
